What it is

The Workflow Field Report is a fixed-price Workflow Review of one operational problem. Applied Process Computing looks at how the work actually happens, where the friction is, what information moves through the process, what tools are involved, and what is worth fixing first.

Workflow review

A focused look at one workflow, reporting workflow, handoff, spreadsheet, or recurring task.

Business process review

A practical way to understand whether the work needs cleanup, documentation, automation, or software.

Improvement options

Clear next-step thinking around process improvement, workflow automation, and internal tools.

Before a build

A low-risk first step before committing to larger custom software or automation work.

This is for you if...

A spreadsheet or report has become too important and too fragile.

Staff repeat the same manual steps every week or month.

A process depends on one person remembering the right sequence.

Information is copied between email, Excel, folders, PDFs, or databases.

Nobody is sure whether the issue needs software, cleanup, automation, or a simpler process.

You want a practical outside review before committing to a larger project.

How the session works

You do not need to know exactly which workflow to choose before contacting Applied Process Computing. Bring the messy list. The session helps narrow the field.

01.

Bring the pain points

Bring several operational problems, recurring tasks, reports, handoffs, or administrative trouble spots.

02.

03.

04.

05.

Common Questions

Do I need to know exactly which workflow to review?

No. You can bring several problems, and the session can help narrow down which one is worth reviewing.

What should I bring to the session?

Bring the current process, reports, spreadsheets, screenshots, forms, handoff notes, or examples that show how the work happens.

Is this only for software projects?

No. The field report may point toward cleanup, documentation, process changes, workflow automation, internal tools, or no build at all.

What happens after the report?

You can use the report internally, request custom software or automation support, or simply use it as a clearer next step. If you move forward with APC within 60 days, $250 may be credited toward qualifying paid work.

Can this lead to custom software or automation work?

Yes, if the review shows that a focused tool, automation, or reporting system would actually help.
